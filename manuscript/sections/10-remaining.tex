\section{The remaining gaps, exhaustively}\label{sec:remaining}

This section is the paper's ledger: Theorem~\ref{thm:conditional} (rung
ii) is claimed modulo exactly the items below, each stated precisely
where cited. No other unproved ingredient is used anywhere.

\cite{TT25} in the $x$-dependent band edges (their Theorem~1.7 handles
the analogous $x^{1/u}$-threshold dependence; verify the general
Theorem~3.1 does too).

\item[(B3)] The verbatim statement of Theorem~3.1 (the original

equidistribution lemma for the interpolation functions $g_z$.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{On Lemma C (standard toolbox; medium risk)}
\begin{itemize}

simultaneous-fractional-parts analysis (Friedlander--Iwaniec; the exact
source is to be pinned). Scheduled after the Lemma-D interface

\subsection*{Trims (lower-bound track unaffected)}
Top of band $u,u'\le\tfrac12-\eta$; corner
$\sqrt R\ge\Llog^{B/2+c+5}$; margin costs. For

(worst-case-per-modulus versions suffice), and a single

\subsection*{Known dead ends (recorded; do not re-enter)}
Union bounds across the digit layer (failure mass $\Theta(1)$);
smooth-cofactor designs ($\rho(c/\delta)$ loses to $e^{-c/\delta}$);
the semiprime trick (dies on the prime case); TT/Pilatte as a black box
(dilation step); crude Cauchy--Schwarz over the mod-$q$ family (discards
the deep twist); Heilbronn completion via max-over-intervals (error
dominates); the citation-adaptation route $\alpha+\beta$ for small
$\lambda$ (Remark~\ref{rem:negative}); single-scale (M2) and the
polylog minor claim (Remark~\ref{rem:M2-strengthening}).
