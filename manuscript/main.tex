\begin{abstract}
Let $\W=\{n\ge2:\ n(n-1)\mid\binom{2n}{n}\}$. We assemble the proof
architecture for the statement that $\W$ has logarithmic density
$c_1^2$, where $c_1=0.11424\ldots$ is the Ford--Konyagin density of the
governor set $\D=\{m:\ m\mid\binom{2m}{m}\}$; even the infinitude of
$\W$ appears to be absent from the literature. The reduction is exact: a
margin-modified sandwich (Lemma~\ref{lem:sandwich}) replaces $\W$ by a
binary correlation of two fixed sets, which then factors through four
independence statements (Lemmas A--D). This draft records, with complete
proofs, the parts of the program that are proved --- the sandwich, the deep
large sieve for Fermat-quotient characters, the arithmetic-progression
opening, the linearization and two-frequency reduction of Estimate
D$^\dagger$, the minor-arc lemma, and the anatomy unwinding --- and isolates
the remaining gaps as precisely stated hypotheses. It is the durable spine
into which the remaining work packages land.
\end{abstract}
